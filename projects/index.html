Argos for NER

This semester I am excited to announce that I will be working as a Software Engineer for the Data Visualization and Analysis Team for Notheastern Electric Racing! We are going to be working on a few different really cool projects to help our engineers access and understand data from the car so that they can better improve it. Currently in development is a project called Argos, essentially a telemetry hub that lets users query any and all data from the car’s server, then organizes and presents it in a user-friendly series of charts and figures.

Routers, Protocols, Crawlers Oh My!

In this post we will be looking at two of my favorite projects from a class that I have really been enjoying recently, Networks and Distributed Systems! The first project we will be looking at is a simplified virtual BGP router. The professors provided us with a simulator and several configs, in which the router we built must be ran against and pass in correctness and performance. Responsibilities of the router included accepting route announcements from simulated peer routers, generating new route announcements for peer routers, managing and compressing forwarding tables, calculating optimal routes, and forwarding data packets that arrive from simulated internet users.
